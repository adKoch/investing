# Discussion: Limits of Screening & True Investing Edge

- **Date**: 2026-06-12
- **Participants**: User, Antigravity (Gemini)

## Summary of Discussion

We addressed a critical question: **If quality investing can be simplified into a stock screener, why hasn't it been commoditized into an ETF, erasing the edge?**

We established that:
1. **Screeners are coarse filters**: Screeners use backward-looking historical financials. They cannot evaluate qualitative moats, management integrity, disruptive technology, or cyclical traps. 
2. **Institutional constraints preserve the edge**: ETFs and large funds are bound by liquidity (cannot buy small-caps), career risk (cannot tolerate short-term underperformance), and index rules.
3. **The true edge is behavioral and analytical**: The edge lies in the qualitative diligence of the business and the temperament to do nothing (hold for years) when the market panic-sells.

## Decisions Made
- Created [screener_limits.md](file:///c:/projects/investing/docs/value/screener_limits.md) to define the limits of quant screens and outline the source of our investing edge.
- Confirmed that our automated screener will be used *only* as a Stage 1 filtering tool, not as an automatic buyer.
