# Sources & Tools for Finding Compounders

To execute a quality investing strategy, an investor needs reliable sources to identify candidate businesses. These sources fall into two main categories: **quantitative tools** (screening databases) and **qualitative sources** (tracking superinvestors and research services).

---

## 1. Quantitative Stock Screeners

Screening is the fastest way to narrow down thousands of global stocks to a shortlist of high-capital-return businesses.

### Top Screener Platforms
*   **TIKR Terminal / Koyfin**: Professional-grade databases providing 10+ years of standardized financial statements. Ideal for analyzing ROIC, ROCE, and free cash flow margins over full economic cycles.
*   **Finchat.io**: AI-assisted financial platform designed specifically for screening fundamental metrics.
*   **GuruFocus**: Offers deep fundamental screening, valuation tools (DCF models), and financial health rankings (Piotroski F-Score, Altman Z-Score).

### Recommended Quality Screener Filters
When setting up a stock screener to find compounders, use these initial filters:

| Metric | Target Threshold | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ROIC / ROCE** | `> 15%` consistently | Verifies high efficiency and profitability of capital. |
| **ROIC Standard Deviation** | Low (stable) over 10 yrs | Consistency indicates a durable moat rather than cyclical spikes. |
| **Gross Margin** | `> 40%` | High gross margins indicate strong pricing power. |
| **Net Debt / EBITDA** | `< 2.5x` | Ensures returns are driven by operational efficiency, not excessive debt. |
| **FCF Conversion** | `> 80%` of Net Income | Confirms accounting profits are backed by actual cash. |

## 2. Qualitative Superinvestor Tracking

Instead of screening the entire market from scratch, investors can look at the portfolios of successful quality-focused fund managers.

### Tracking Portfolios (13F Filings)
*   **Dataroma / WhaleWisdom**: Websites that aggregate SEC 13F filings, showing exactly what stocks top fund managers are buying, selling, and holding.
*   **Target Portfolios to Watch**:
    *   *Akre Capital Management* (Chuck Akre)
    *   *Fundsmith* (Terry Smith)
    *   *Lindsell Train* (Nick Train)
    *   *Nomad Investment Partnership* (Nick Sleep - historical letters)
    *   *Sequoia Fund* (Ruane, Cunniff & Goldfarb)
    *   *Berkshire Hathaway* (Warren Buffett)

## 3. Curated Moat Indices and Research

Several research organizations pre-screen the market for wide-moat companies.

*   **Morningstar Wide Moat Index**: Tracks companies that Morningstar’s analysts designate as having "Wide Moats" (durable advantages likely to last 20+ years) trading at reasonable valuations.
*   **Value Line Investment Survey**: One of the oldest research newsletters, known for its "Timeliness" and "Safety" rankings, which favor high-quality, stable companies.

---

## 4. Value Investing Communities

Independent analysts often share deep-dive reports on wide-moat compounders on online platforms:
*   **Value Investors Club (VIC)**: An invite-only forum where buyside analysts present detailed investment theses. Past write-ups are publicly available after a short delay.
*   **Specialized Substacks**: Many independent researchers publish newsletters dedicated specifically to compounders (e.g., *Compounding Quality*, *Quality Stocks*).
