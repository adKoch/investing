# Discussion: Charlie Munger's Quality Investing Philosophy

- **Date**: 2026-06-12
- **Participants**: User, Antigravity (Gemini)

## Summary of Discussion

We started our first strategy discussion, inspired by the user's reading of *Poor Charlie's Almanack* by Charlie Munger. 

The core thesis discussed is that **beating the market can be achieved by finding and investing in businesses of above-average quality**. Rather than focusing solely on "cheapness" (traditional cigar-butt value investing), Munger's approach focuses on finding "wonderful businesses at fair prices" and letting them compound over long periods.

### Key Principles Explored:
1. **High-Quality Businesses**: A company with high returns on capital, strong pricing power, and durable competitive positions.
2. **The Power of Compounding**: A business that can reinvest its earnings at high rates of return will produce massive wealth over time.
3. **Moats**: Durable competitive advantages that protect high margins from being eroded by competitors.
4. **Latticework of Mental Models**: Using multi-disciplinary thinking to assess quality, incentives, and risks.
