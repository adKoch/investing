## Summary of Discussion

We discussed whether being located in Poland/Europe offers any structural investing advantages, and if we should consider European and Polish (GPW) stocks.

We identified three key advantages:
1. **The GPW Inefficiency Edge**: Smaller developing markets like the GPW (Warsaw Stock Exchange) are ignored by large institutional quants due to liquidity constraints. This allows individual investors to acquire high-ROIC compounders (like Dino Polska) at highly inefficient prices.
2. **European "Hidden Champions"**: Europe contains thousands of highly specialized, family-controlled mid-caps that focus on long-term capital compounding and low debt rather than quarterly earnings pressure.
3. **Tax Arbitrage (IKE/IKZE)**: Local Polish tax wrappers (IKE/IKZE) shield portfolios from the 19% capital gains tax ("podatek Belki"), maximizing compounding math over long-term holding periods.
